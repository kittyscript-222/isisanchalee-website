Yogic Dictionary

sādhana

/ SAH-dha-na /

A daily spiritual practice — the disciplined inner path through which the practitioner moves toward liberation and self-realization.

saṅgha

/ SUNG-ha /

A community of practitioners walking the path together — the sacred power of collective intention and shared devotion.

Mahāśivarātri

/ ma-HAH-shee-va-RAH-tree /

The Great Night of Shiva — the most auspicious night of the yogic calendar, considered the single most powerful night of the year for spiritual practice.

nāda yoga

/ NAH-da YO-ga /

The yoga of sound — a practice of using sacred vibration, mantra and chanting as a direct path to inner stillness and expanded states of consciousness.

parikramā

/ pah-ree-KRAH-mah /

Sacred circumambulation — the devotional act of walking barefoot clockwise around a holy site, temple, or body of water as an act of reverence and purification.

The Vision

Sādhana, creative expression
& the magic of saṅgha

Pushkar is one of the rarest and holiest cities in all of India. It is home to the only Brahma temple in the world and is surrounding a sacred lake that has been a site of pilgrimage for thousands of years.

At its heart, this is a sādhana and creative expression retreat — designed in a way for your inner artist to bloom. Through deep practice we cultivate the inner conditions of stillness, clarity and deep intuitive connection from which authentic creative expression naturally arises. From there we will have workshops for devotional poetry, painting, singing kirtan and learning traditional Kalbeliya dance with Rajasthani folk artists to let your creative channels flourish.

We have planned the retreat to build up to the most auspicious night of the yogic calendar — Mahashivaratri, the Great Night of Shiva. It is known to the yogis that this is the single-most powerful night of the entire year to receive the most merits from your spiritual practice. We will join together for a large puja in celebration and see what other magic awaits in Pushkar.

This is a retreat for sincere seekers who want real depth — not a holiday with a yoga class. Be prepared to go deep, have fun, and most-likely meet your new best friends in the process.

The Programme

Ten days of sacred immersion

Each day is woven with practice, pilgrimage, learning, and deep play. This is the full tapestry of what awaits you.

Daily Practice

Daily Morning Sadhana

Each morning begins with a 2-hour sadhana — 1 hour of classical Hatha pranayama to create the conditions for deep meditation, followed by 30 minutes of stotram chanting and 30 minutes of Naad Yoga. Don't expect fancy asanas, this is yoga as the path to expansion & liberation.

Sacred Arts

Stotram Chanting

Learn to chant the Nirvana Shatakam and the Panchakshara Stotram in their full form, alongside daily practice of Shiva Japa. They are precise vibrational technologies — sacred frequencies that purify the mind and open the heart from the inside out.

Living Tradition

Learn Kalbeliya Dance

The Kalbeliya are the nomadic snake-charmer community of Rajasthan, whose traditional folk dance is a UNESCO-recognised art form and an act of living devotion. We dance with them under the desert stars, serenaded by traditional Rajasthani folk musicians — discovering the liberation of embodied, uninhibited joy in one of the most alive settings on earth.

Creative Practice

Poetry Writing Workshop

The sacred lands of India are famous for their devotional poets — Mirabai, Kabir, Rumi, Utpaladeva. Seated on the ghats of Pushkar Lake we will be guided through a workshop to connect with the Beloved and channel devotional poetry from the deepest place within.

Pilgrimage

Pilgrimage to Sacred Sites

We will take you to some of the most sacred sites in the region — the Brahma temple, through the ghats, and hilltop shrines from where sacred waters flow.

Sacred Ritual

Parikrama Around Pushkar Lake

The parikrama — circumambulation — of the sacred Pushkar Lake is a purifying practice believed to cleanse lifetimes of karma. We walk it together at sunset, in silence, as the golden light fades over the water and the sound of temple bells fills the air.

Sacred Fire

Ganesha Havan

We begin our journey with a Ganesha Havan — a sacred fire ceremony with a Vedic Priest invoking the remover of obstacles to bless and clear obstacles from our path.

Cosmic Ritual

Navagraha Havan

This is for all of our astrology girlies. The Navagraha Havan — nine planetary fire ceremony — is one of the most powerful Vedic rituals for clearing karmic imprints and bringing the nine planetary forces into harmonious alignment.

Creative Practice

Painting Class

Painting as meditation, as offering and devotion. A guided session where the canvas becomes a space to express from the depths of our hearts what words and movement cannot reach.

Rest & Restoration

Spa Day

After the all-night Mahashivaratri vigil, we have a full day of rest and restoration — spa time, self-care and deep self-nourishment. We will take it easy, move slow, and allow ourselves time to integrate the powerful energies from the night before.

Deep Rest

Yoga Nidra

The ancient practice of yogic sleep that brings the body and nervous system into profound rest while the mind remains aware. One of the most powerful tools for integration, restoration, and accessing the subconscious.

Devotional Music

Kirtan

The ancient art of bhakti yoga — singing the names of the divine together. Be prepared to open your voice and your heart.

Community & Expression

Open Mic Night

One evening at the end of our time together we gather and offer our creative expression — a song, a poem, a dance, a piece of writing, or whatever is alive in your heart. No performance required, just the courage to share what the journey has moved in you.

Creative Practice

Photoshoot & Content Creation

A dedicated photoshoot session at sunset on the magical ghats of Pushkar Lake. Gina's lens will capture you in the fullness of who you are becoming. Leave with beautiful images that reflects the depth, beauty and devotion of your journey.

The Centrepiece · The Great Night

Mahashivaratri Puja & Celebrations

The culmination of our entire retreat. Together we perform a large puja for Mahashivaratri at a Vedic Priest's house, and then surrender into the magic of Pushkar's Mahashivaratri celebrations. We will chant, pray, dance, and stay up all night in deep devotion.

FAQ

Your questions

Do I need prior experience with yoga or sadhana?
A genuine longing to go deeper is the only prerequisite. Some participants will be experienced practitioners; others will be arriving at their first encounter with classical yogic tradition. All are welcome. The practices are taught from the ground up, and the land and the ceremonies themselves are the greatest teachers.
How do I get to Pushkar?
Fly into Jaipur International Airport (JAI) — the nearest major airport, approximately 2.5 hours from Pushkar. We recommend arriving in Jaipur the day before the retreat begins. Transfer to Pushkar by taxi or bus. We will share detailed travel guidance upon booking.
Is this appropriate for someone new to India?
Yes. We hold the container carefully, providing guidance on how to move through the culture with respect and openness. That said, India is not always comfortable — it is destabilising in the most beautiful way. Come expecting your world to be expanded, not catered to.
What is the refund / cancellation policy?
The $800 deposit is non-refundable in all circumstances but is transferable to a future retreat with 60+ days notice. Cancellations within 60 days of the retreat start date forfeit 50% of the total amount paid. We strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance covering cancellation.
What should I bring?
Light, modest cotton clothing suitable for temples and pilgrimage (covered shoulders and knees). A shawl or pashmina for cool desert evenings and morning sadhana. Comfortable walking shoes. A journal. An open heart. A full packing list is sent upon booking confirmation.
